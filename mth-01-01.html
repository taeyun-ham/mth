Chapter Two: The Monster

Jack crawled into the tree house.
“Wow,” he said. The tree house was filled with books.
Books were everywhere—very old books with dusty covers and new shiny, bright covers.
“Look,” said Annie. “You can see far away.”
She was peering out the window.
Jack looked out the window with her.
Below were the tops of the other trees.
In the distance he could see the Frog Creek library, elementary school, and park.
Annie pointed in the other direction.
“There’s our house,” she said.
Jack could see their white wooden house with a green porch.
In the yard was their neighbor’s black dog.
“Shush! We’re not supposed to be up here,” said Jack.
Jack glanced around.
He noticed that bookmarks were sticking out of many books.
“I wonder who owns all these books.”
She picked up a book with a castle on the cover.
“Here’s a book about Pennsylvania,” said Jack.
He turned to the page with the bookmark.
“Here’s a picture of Frog Creek… of these woods!”
She held up a book about dinosaurs. A blue silk bookmark was sticking out.
“Let me see.” Jack grabbed the book.
He turned to a picture of an ancient flying reptile.
He touched the huge bat-like wings.
“I wish we could go to the time of Pteranodons.”
He studied the picture of the odd-looking creature soaring through the sky.
“Ahhh!” screamed Annie.
“A monster!” she cried.
Jack looked out. A giant creature was gliding above the treetops.
It had a long, weird crest, a skinny beak, and huge bat-like wings.
It was a real live Pteranodon!
It swooped through the sky like a glider plane.
It was coming straight toward the tree house!
“Get down!” cried Annie.
They crouched on the floor.
The wind started to blow.
The tree house started to spin.
It spun faster and faster.
Then everything was still. Absolutely still.
